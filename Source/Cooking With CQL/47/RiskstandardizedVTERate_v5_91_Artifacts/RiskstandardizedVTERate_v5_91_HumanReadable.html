eCQM Title

V2_Risk-standardized Venous Thromboembolism (VTE) Rate Following Elective Primary Total Hip Arthroplasty (THA) and/or Total Knee Arthroplasty (TKA) Electronic Clinical Quality Measure (eCQM)
eCQM Identifier (Measure Authoring Tool) 993 eCQM Version Number 0.0.036
NQF Number Not Applicable GUID 0e673c98-4d6d-45dd-bf4c-931cedfb22e2
Measurement Period January 1, 20XX through December 31, 20XX
Measure Steward Brigham and Womens Health
Measure Developer Brigham and Womens Health
Endorsed By None
Description 
This measure estimates the combined risk-standardized major bleeding and VTE rate following elective primary THA/TKA at the clinician group level. The outcome is defined as any major bleeding and/or VTE event occurring from the date of the THA/TKA procedure to 35 days postdate of procedure (or procedure encounter if the procedure is done on an outpatient basis). Because this is a Merit-based Incentivized Payment System (MIPS) measure, the target population is patients 18 years and older across all payers.

			Risk Adjustment
	     	
      			

       				
In order to account for differences in case mix among hospitals, the measure includes an adjustment for factors such as age, comorbid diseases, and indicators of patient frailty, which are clinically relevant and have relationships with the outcome. For each patient, risk-adjustment variables are obtained from inpatient, outpatient, and physician Medicare administrative claims data extending 12 months prior to the index admission, and all claims data for the index admission itself.

The measure’s adjustment for case mix differences among hospitals is based on the clinical status of the patient at the time of the index admission. Accordingly, only 2019 Measure Updates: THA/TKA Complication 13 comorbidities that convey information about the patient at the time of the index admission, or any time within the preceding 12 months, and not complications that arise during the course of the hospitalization, are included in risk adjustment.

The measure does not include an adjustment for socioeconomic status (SES) because the association between SES and health outcomes can be due, in part, to differences in the quality of health care that groups of patients with varying SES receive. The intent is for the measure to adjust for patient demographic and clinical characteristics while illuminating important quality differences. The National Quality Forum (NQF) re-endorsed the measure without adjustment for patient-level SES factors.

In addition to the Suplemental Data Elements, we have added Comorbidity, BMI and smoking status as risk factors.

			Rationale
	     	
 				

       				
Composite Measure Rationale: This proposed measure addresses the CMS Quality Priority “making care safer by reducing harm caused by the delivery of care” and CMS’s preventable measure area of “Preventable Healthcare Harm”. This outcome-based measure will promote quality improvements without creating burden to the clinicians by using routinely documented EHR data. Because this eCQM will include risk adjustment, it has the potential to identify clinician groups whose performance is better or worse than would be expected on a clinician group’s patient case mix, which will ultimately inform consumers about the care quality and guide their choice in surgical group practice. A measure that reports both risk-standardized VTE and major bleeding rates is patient-centered in that it will assist healthcare providers to personalize care by identifying the appropriate VTE prophylactic regimen that balances each patient’s risk of VTE (from under coagulation) and risk of major bleeding (from over coagulation). Furthermore, it will strengthen incentives for quality improvement, and ultimately improve the quality of care received by patients, regardless of payer. By providing ongoing patient outcome data that can be used in near real time to improve practice, this proposed eCQM has the potential to lower healthcare costs associated with VTE and major bleeding following elective primary THA and TKA. 

The measure estimates the sum of clinician group-level risk standardized VTE rate and major bleeding rate following elective primary THA/TKA using hierarchical logistic regression models. In brief, the approach simultaneously models data at the patient and clinician group levels to account for variance in patient outcomes within and between clinician groups (Normand et al., 2007; Dimick et al., 2010;  Krell et al., 2014;  MacKenzie et al., 2015). At the patient level, it models the log-odds of a VTE event and/or bleeding event occurring during the index admission or within 35 days from surgery using age, sex, selected clinical covariates and a clinician group-specific random intercept. At the clinician group level, it models the clinician group-specific intercepts as arising from a normal distribution. The clinician group intercept represents the underlying risk of a VTE or major bleeding event at the clinician group level, after accounting for patient risk. The clinician group-specific intercepts are given a distribution to account for the clustering (non-independence) of patients within the same clinician group. If there were no differences among clinician groups, then after adjusting for patient risk, the clinician group intercepts should be identical across all clinician groups.  

The risk-standardized VTE and/or major bleeding rate is calculated as the ratio of the number of “predicted” to the number of “expected” episodes with a VTE and/or major bleeding event, multiplied by the sum of national observed rates for VTE and major bleeding following TJA. For each clinician group, the numerator of the ratio is the number of VTE and/or bleeding events within 35 days predicted based on the clinician group’s performance with its observed case mix, and the denominator is the number of VTE and/or bleeding events expected based on the nation’s performance with that clinician group’s case mix. This approach is analogous to a ratio of “observed” to “expected” used in other types of statistical analyses. It conceptually allows for a comparison of a clinician group’s performance given its case mix to an average clinician group’s performance with the same case mix. Thus, a lower ratio indicates lower-than-expected bleeding event rates or better quality, and a higher ratio indicates higher-than-expected bleeding event rates or worse quality.

The target population for the publicly reported measure includes admissions for adults 18 years of age or older undergoing elective primary THA and/or TKA procedures.

      			

  					
		


			

				Denominator
		     	
	 				

	       				
Equals Initial Population.

	      			

  						
			


			

				Denominator Exclusions
		     	
	 				

	       				
1.      Patients who were discharged against medical advice (AMA)

2.      Patients who had more than two THA/TKA procedure codes during the index hospitalization

3.      Patients with diagnosis codes for renal insufficiency within the 365 days prior to the THA/TKA procedure

4.      Patients with diagnosis codes for chronic atrial fibrillation within the 365 days prior to the THA/TKA procedure

5.      Patients with diagnosis codes for cancer within the 365 days prior to the THA/TKA procedure

6.      Who received prescription orders for anticoagulant medications 10-90 Days Prior to Surgery and meets the following criteria:
         •       Patient who received an Anticoagulant Injection/Infusion
         •       Patient who received a tablet (Oral) Anticoagulant order, Quantity > 1

7.      Patients with diagnosis code for VTE or DVT within 30 days prior to the index admission

8.      Patients with diagnosis code for coagulation disorder within 365 prior to the THA/TKA procedure

9.      Diagnosed With Excluded Conditions Within 90 Days Prior To The Index Admission

10.    Patients who had additional surgery within 35 days from the elective primary THA/TKA (TBD)

	      			

  						
			





			

				Numerator
		     	
	 				

	       				
A patient will be in the numerator for this measure if they have a VTE event occurring from the date of THA/TKA procedure to 35 days postdate of procedure.
